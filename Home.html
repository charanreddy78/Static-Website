Types of seasons

Kharif and Rabi are two distinct cropping seasons in India that are based on the monsoon cycle:

Kharif season

Also known as the summer or monsoon season, Kharif crops are sown with the onset of the southwest monsoon in June-July and harvested in September-October. The crops grown during the Kharif season require a lot of water and include crops like rice, maize, millets, cotton, soybeans, and groundnuts. These crops are typically sown in the rainy season and are dependent on the monsoon for irrigation.

Rabi season

Also known as the winter season, Rabi crops are sown in October-November after the monsoon season ends and are harvested in March-April. These crops are grown in areas where irrigation facilities are available or where there is residual moisture left by the monsoon rains. Rabi crops include wheat, barley, oats, chickpeas (chana), mustard, and peas.
